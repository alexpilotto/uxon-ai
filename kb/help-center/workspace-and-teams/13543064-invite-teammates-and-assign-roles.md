## Article
You can invite colleagues to your organization and control what they can access by assigning roles.

### Organization owner vs Admin

The person who creates the organization becomes the **Owner**.

The Owner and **Admin** roles have nearly the same level of access. Both can manage the entire organization, including teams, billing, and sub-accounts.

The key difference:

- Only the **Owner** holds ownership of the organization.
- An **Admin** cannot take ownership or delete the organization.

If you need to change the organization owner, contact support via live chat.

### User roles and access levels

UXON AI supports three roles:

| Role | Access |
| --- | --- |
| **Admin** | Has full control of the organization but does not own it and cannot delete it. Can manage billing, subscriptions, invoices, teams, and all sub-accounts. |
| **Manager** | Can access all sub-accounts and teams and view usage data, but cannot manage billing or financial settings. |
| **Member** | Can only access specific sub-accounts assigned to them. No access to teams, usage, or financial settings. |

### How to invite a team member

1. Click the **Members** indicator in the header near the sub-account selector.
2. Select **Add New Member**.
3. Enter the user's name and email address.
4. Select a role.
5. If the role is **Member**, choose the sub-accounts they can access.
6. Send the invitation.

### Notes

- Members must be assigned at least one sub-account, otherwise they will not be able to join the organization.
- Member roles are useful for compartmentalizing access and limiting visibility across teams and sub-accounts.
